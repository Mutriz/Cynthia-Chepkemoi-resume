National Aids Control Council | Certificate of Merit

  • Cynthia Chepkemoi

The National AIDS Control Council (NACC) in Kenya is a government agency responsible for coordinating the national response to HIV and AIDS. The council was established in 1999 under the State Corporations Act, with the primary objective of providing policy and strategic framework for the country's response to the HIV epidemic. Here are some key points about the National AIDS Control Council in Kenya: Mandate: The NACC is mandated to provide policy and strategy formulation, harmonize and coordinate the multi-sectoral response to HIV and AIDS in Kenya. Coordination: The council plays a central role in coordinating various government ministries, departments, and agencies, as well as non-governmental organizations, development partners, and the private sector involved in HIV and AIDS programs. Strategic Planning: NACC is responsible for developing and implementing the National AIDS Strategic Plan (NASP), which serves as a comprehensive guide for the country's response to HIV and AIDS. Resource Mobilization: The council is involved in resource mobilization efforts to ensure adequate funding for HIV and AIDS programs. This includes both domestic and international funding sources. Monitoring and Evaluation: NACC is responsible for monitoring and evaluating the progress of HIV and AIDS interventions in the country. This involves tracking key indicators, assessing program effectiveness, and ensuring accountability. Advocacy and Awareness: NACC engages in advocacy efforts to raise awareness about HIV and AIDS, reduce stigma and discrimination, and promote behavior change through targeted communication strategies. Research and Data Management: The council is involved in coordinating research activities related to HIV and AIDS and managing data collection, analysis, and dissemination to inform evidence-based decision-making. Partnerships: NACC collaborates with various stakeholders, including international organizations, civil society groups, and communities affected by HIV and AIDS, to strengthen the overall response. Legal and Policy Framework: NACC works on developing and updating legal and policy frameworks related to HIV and AIDS, ensuring that they are aligned with international best practices and human rights principles. The National AIDS Control Council in Kenya plays a crucial role in ensuring a comprehensive and effective response to the HIV epidemic in the country. The council's work is integral to achieving national and global goals related to HIV prevention, treatment, care, and support.
