PAN-AFRICAN YOUTH AMBASSADORS FOR INTERNET GOVERNANCE 2023 (PAYAIG) – SWAHILI FACILITATOR

March 2023 – November 2023

2023 IGF focused on the theme "The Internet We Want - Empowering All People". On this project I worked with PAYAIG as a Swahili Facilitator/Trainer for the Swahili Cohort Fellows. I took fellows through the topic on "Utangulizi wa Utawa Wa Mtandao (Introduction to Internet Governance)" and "Masuala ya Kisheria na Kiuchumi karika Utawala wa Mtandao (Legal and Economic Issues in Internet Governance)" The PAYAIG Initiative, developed by Cyber Czar and generously backed by the UK Government, in collaboration with AUC PRIDA, IGF Secretariat, as well as several regional NRIs and IGF stakeholders, successfully empowered over 1000 young Africans between the ages of 18-30 to engage actively and meaningfully in internet governance discussions and foster Africa’s internet governance ecosystem.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Developed and updated training materials and curriculum on Internet Governance, considering the latest trends, policies, and technologies.
  • Assessed the knowledge and skill gaps of participants to tailor training content and delivery methods accordingly.
  • Conducted interactive and engaging training sessions, workshop on Internet Governance topics.
  • Fostered an inclusive learning environment that encourages participant involvement and discussion through pop-up quizzes and polls.
  • Stayed updated on trends relating to global Internet Governance issues, policies, and best practices to provide accurate and relevant information.
  • Educated participants on national and international policies and regulations related to Internet Governance in the first module on “Introduction to Internet Governance”
  • Integrate real-world case studies and practical examples to illustrate the application of Internet Governance principles across the Global North with a key focus on East Africa.
  • Facilitated networking opportunities and collaborative activities among participants to encourage knowledge sharing across the regional and in-country Internet Governance Forums.
  • Emphasized the importance of a multi-stakeholder approach in Internet Governance and provide insights into the roles of various stakeholders such as Civil Society, Research and Academia, Government, Technical Community and Private Sector.
  • Contributed to the development of participants' skills and competencies in understanding, managing, and influencing Internet Governance processes through policy development and public participation.
  • Encouraged participants to engage in policy advocacy and contribute to discussions on shaping Internet Governance policies.
  • Solicited feedback through use of survey forms from participants to continuously improve training content, methodologies, and delivery.
  • Developed and delivered remote training sessions using online platforms, considering the global nature of Internet Governance discussion.
  • Supported awareness campaigns by creating awareness about the use of community networks to educate the broader community about the importance of Internet Governance.
  • Prepared evaluation questions to measure the effectiveness of the training program and made necessary adjustments for improvement.
  • Emphasize ethical considerations, privacy, cyber hygiene and security concerns related to Internet Governance in training sessions.
  • Documented training materials, session notes, and participant feedback for future reference and improvement.
  • Ensured that training materials and sessions are inclusive and consider diverse perspectives.
