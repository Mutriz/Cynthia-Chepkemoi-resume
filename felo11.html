The program is an outgrowth of the Research Group, and includes requirements for research, writing and policy analysis. CAIDP offers policy clinics, which are intensive, interdisciplinary, semester-long courses, established to teach future leaders in the AI policy field skills in policy analysis, research, evaluation, team management, and policy formation. The topics covered during the program include: AI History, AI issues and Institutions, AI Regulation and Research Methods. Particular attention is given to the AI policy framework that are shaping international norm for the use of AI. These include the Universal Guidelines for AI (2018), the OECD AI Principles/G20 AI Guidelines (2019), the UNESCO Recommendations on the Ethics of AI (2021), the US Blueprint for an AI Bill of Rights (2022), the Eau AI Act (2023), and the Council of Europe Convention on AI.
